## Methodology grounding

The wheel follows Jerome Glenn's canonical Futures Wheel methodology
(1972; see Swanson 2020 and Kemp 2025 for contemporary summaries):
a central driver radiates outward through first-, second-, and
third-order consequences as a radial mind map.

The §2.1 narrative MUST open with a sentence citing Glenn as the
method's origin.

This wheel extends the canonical format in two ways. The narrative
MUST disclose both as deliberate extensions, NOT as standard
methodology — honest framing is the point:

- **Extension 1 — third-order consequences are evaluated against a
  "systemic restructuring" criterion** rather than treated as merely
  intensified second-order effects. Justification: Voros's (2003)
  Futures Cone literature distinguishes scenarios by category-creating
  shifts; we apply the same criterion at the wheel's outer ring to
  preserve a clean handoff into §2.3 (Cone of Plausibility).

- **Extension 2 — third-order consequences are tagged to the Cone
  scenarios in §2.3.** This is a research-to-artifact cohesion device
  aligned with the project brief's grading rubric, not a Glenn
  requirement.

Phrase these as "we extend the standard format by..." in the narrative.
