# §2.1 Futures Wheel — Output

## Section 1 — §2.1 Futures Wheel Narrative

This Futures Wheel follows the canonical methodology in which a central driver radiates outward through first-, second-, and third-order consequences as a radial mind map, with each consequence assigned to a single parent in the preceding ring. The driver at the center is the dating industry's 2027–2030 de-monetization wall: ARPU declines across every subsector while user count grows (Statista, *Dating Services — Worldwide*, 2026); EU AI Act Articles 5(1)(b), 27, and 50 expose engagement-on-loneliness algorithms to legal liability "by effect" and impose deployer-side compliance burden (Regulation 2024/1689); and the median user ages past the 20–35 product window toward 35.21 by 2046 (UN World Population Prospects 2024). We extend the standard wheel format in one deliberate way: third-order consequences are evaluated against a "systemic restructuring" criterion drawn from the Futures Cone framework's emphasis on category-creating shifts, rather than treated as intensified second-order effects — this preserves a clean handoff into §2.3. Each third-order node is tagged to a Cone scenario as a research-to-artifact cohesion device.

The first ring is STEEP-balanced rather than linear; the five direct consequences deliberately compete. [A] *AI-companion substitution* is the technological branch — but the demographic structure complicates a simple substitution narrative. Between 2023 and 2024, 65.4% of AI companion app users were aged 18–24, with users over 50 accounting for only 13.7% combined (Statista / Appfigures 2024). The dating-app median user, meanwhile, is aging toward 35 (UN WPP 2024). The two populations run in parallel rather than sequence: a generationally distinct cohort adopts AI companions as default intimacy infrastructure, while the legacy dating-app cohort ages out. This generational misalignment feeds Ring 3's stratification consequence (node 3) along a second axis beyond income and jurisdiction. [B] *Outdoor sociality compresses* under environmental and spatial pressure, with 150–300 dangerous heat-humidity days per year projected for target megacities under SSP2-4.5 to SSP3-7.0 (IPCC 2023, SPM Figure SPM.3; see also Xue, Chen, and Yu 2025 on sustainable aviation trajectories). [C] An *EU regulatory floor on AI-mediated intimacy* emerges from Article 50 disclosure obligations, Article 5(1)(b)'s "by effect" prohibition on exploitation of vulnerability, and Article 27's deployer-side Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment requirement (Regulation 2024/1689). [D] Economically, *surviving platforms consolidate post-wall into a barbell of tiered jurisdictional pricing* — premium verified-human against cheap unverified AI (Statista 2026 revenue-wall forecast). [E] Socially, *the dating lifespan lengthens*: median age 31→35 and a 49% rise in the old-age dependency ratio — both demographic shifts within the social dimension — break the 20–35 product logic (UN WPP 2024).

The second ring traces reactive forces, not smooth extrapolation. Each Ring 2 node is assigned to a single Ring 1 parent based on primary causation, with secondary interaction effects noted in node captions. Branch C generates four Ring 2 consequences along divergent paths: under a holding Brussels Effect, EU regulation legitimates AI companionship across roughly 38 states and 71% of the dating market (Bradford 2020); under fragmentation, the same regulatory pressure drives engagement-optimization into offshore gray-market AI (Bradford 2020, inverse case); the compliance burden of Articles 27 and 50 displaces small operators in favor of incumbents able to absorb FRIA documentation and disclosure costs (Regulation 2024/1689); and the resulting disclosure regime creates the legal substrate for an authenticated-human premium product tier serving affluent older cohorts (Regulation 2024/1689; Statista 2026). Branch B's heat compression forces indoor commercial venues to become the substrate for intimate sociality (IPCC 2023), and within branch B aviation's growth trajectory consumes nearly a quarter of the 1.5°C carbon budget by 2050 (Plucinska 2025; Dobruszkes, Mattioli, and Gozzoli 2024), making long-distance romantic mobility a contested climate-policy variable that bites asymmetrically along the income lines already established by branch D. Branch D's barbell consolidates the mid-market collapse (Statista 2026). Branch E reframes loneliness from individual complaint to population-health framing as working-age cohorts manage longer dating lifespans under heavier care burden (UN WPP 2024; IPCC 2023, SPM A.2.5 flags mental-health load with high confidence; Khatib 2023 on climate-travel health intersections).

Six third-order consequences pass the systemic-restructuring test, each assigned to a single Ring 2 parent. (1) *Intimacy infrastructure becomes a public-goods question* (parent: D1) — the mid-market collapse is the trigger that forces the public-or-private fork; physical demand from B1 and compliance pressure from C1 condition but do not cause the question (→ Projected + Preferred, the same question with opposite answers). (2) *The dating industry merges with the care economy* (parent: E2) — loneliness reframed as a population-health issue is the precondition for doctor-prescribed, insurance-covered intimacy infrastructure; CE1's verified-human tier is a parallel commercialization path, not the cause of the merger (→ Preferred — Care Commons). (3) *Two-tier intimacy stratification by jurisdiction and income* (parent: D1) — the barbell market structure is stratification itself; A2's offshore AI and CE1's premium tier are the content filling the two ends, not the cause of the split (→ Projected — Tiered World; Preposterous — Two-Tier World). (4) *Climate-adaptive indoor commons replace open-air sociality* (parent: B1) — outdoor unavailability is the physical necessity; ownership form (privatized via C1, public-cooperative via A1, gray-market via A2) is determined by the regulatory and market context but the commons themselves exist because of B1 (→ all three cones at different ownership switches). (5) *Chronic loneliness reclassified as public-health infrastructure* (parent: E2) — direct extension of the population-health reframing (→ Preferred — Care Commons). (6) *Aviation collapse contracts the geography of romance*, pushing cross-regional intimacy into VR/simulation (parent: BD1), with Santoso et al. 2025 showing VR reduces psychological distance to remote locations (→ Preposterous — Two-Tier World, anchored in the Companion Wars wildcard from §2.3).

The wheel maps the driver across all three cones; the Connection Club artifact occupies the Preposterous branch, specifically the intersection of node 3 (stratification, via D1) and node 6 (aviation collapse + mediated cross-regional intimacy, via BD1). Climate did not cause the stratification — climate forced the question, and the regulatory answer at each fork decides which cone the wheel resolves into.
