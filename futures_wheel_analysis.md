# Futures Wheel Analysis & Revision Prompt

## Task
Analyze the existing Futures Wheel from the group's Miro board (described 
below) and propose revisions that:
1. Make it methodology-compliant (clear 1st/2nd/3rd order labeling)
2. Anchor each consequence to a research source from our Bibliography
3. Connect the wheel's 3rd-order consequences to our three Cone scenarios
4. Avoid linear extrapolation (Monica explicitly warned against this)

## Existing Wheel Structure (from Miro)

**Center (driver):** "People no longer use dating apps or technology to 
form connections."

**1st-order branches (direct consequences):**
- People rely on in-person interactions to form relationships
- Loss of algorithmic filtering and efficiency in dating
- Social and physical environments become central to connection
- Cultural shift toward authentic and unmediated relationships

**2nd-order branches (reactive consequences):**
- From "in-person interactions": increased vulnerability/risk of rejection; 
  stronger reliance on social skills and confidence
- From "loss of algorithmic filtering": people meet outside their usual 
  "type"; harder for introverted/marginalized people
- From "social/physical environments central": rise in intentionally 
  designed social spaces (cafes, lounges, events); greater importance of 
  circulation, visibility, chance encounters
- From "cultural shift toward authentic": less control over first 
  impressions; relationships form more slowly and unpredictably

**3rd-order branches (systemic consequences):**
- Stronger, more intentional relationships
- Increased social anxiety or avoidance
- Social inequality increases based on access to spaces and communities
- Connection becomes dependent on location, privilege, and social networks
- Interior design and architecture actively shape how people connect
- Raises questions about whether connection is natural or influenced by design

## Methodology requirements (from Monica)

1. **Driver must be grounded in research**, not fiction. Current driver 
   ("people no longer use dating apps") needs to be backed by:
   - Statista ARPU decline + revenue wall data (de-monetization pattern)
   - EU AI Act Article 5(1)(b) making engagement-optimization legally 
     exposed
   - UN WPP demographic shift breaking 20-35 product assumptions
   This makes the driver an extrapolation of observable trends, not pure 
   speculation.

2. **No linear extrapolation**. Don't just take "people stop using apps" 
   and project 20 years forward. Branches must show competing forces, 
   regulatory backlash, second-order systemic responses — including ones 
   that contradict or complicate the central driver.

3. **3rd-order branches must be category-creating** (systemic 
   restructuring), not just intensified versions of 2nd-order. Examples 
   of category-creating: "intimacy infrastructure becomes a public-goods 
   question," "the dating industry merges with the care economy."

4. **Each branch should have a source citation** where possible, drawn 
   from existing Bibliography:
   - IPCC AR6 (climate)
   - UN WPP 2024 (demographics)
   - UN WUP 2018 (urbanization)
   - EU AI Act 2024/1689 (regulation)
   - Statista Dating Services Worldwide (market)
   - Statista AI Companion App Users (technology)
   - Bradford 2020 (Brussels Effect)

5. **Connect to Cone scenarios** (§2.3 of compendium):
   - PROJECTED (Tiered World)
   - PREFERRED (Care Commons)
   - PREPOSTEROUS (Two-Tier World)
   Each 3rd-order consequence should be tagged with which cone(s) it 
   flows into.

## Analysis I need from you

For each branch in the existing wheel:

1. Is the consequence **logically derived** from the parent, or is it 
   linear extrapolation?
2. What **research source** (from Bibliography) could anchor this 
   consequence?
3. Is the 3rd-order consequence **category-creating** or just intensified 
   2nd-order?
4. Which **cone scenario** does this consequence flow into?

Then propose:
- Which branches to **keep as-is**
- Which branches to **rewrite** (and the proposed rewrite)
- Which branches to **remove** (if linear/unsupported)
- Which branches to **add** (if methodology requires it)
