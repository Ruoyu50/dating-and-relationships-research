## Signal 1
### Sector entering maturity

**Fact**: Online dating revenue change dropped from 14.9% in 2018 to ~2% by 2027–2030.

**Interpretation**: A typical late-stage S-curve. The industry has entered saturation.

**Source**: Statista — Dating Services market data & analysis (Feb 2023)
**Corresponding chart**: Chart 04 — online-dating revenue change

## Signal 2
### COVID general slowdown ≠ structural reshuffling

**Fact**: M and CD growth rates dropped sharply in 2020; OD dropped sharply in 2021. However, all three subsectors continued to grow in absolute terms, with share migration of only 4–7pp over 13 years.

**Interpretation**: COVID was a general slowdown event, not a structural reshuffling. All three subsectors grew; OD only took a small share from M.

**Source**: Statista — Dating Services market data & analysis (Feb 2023)
**Corresponding chart**: Chart 01 — dating-services revenue (stacked column + line)
**Secondary evidence**: Charts 03 / 06 / 07 — three revenue lines compared
**Corresponding file**: `raw_capture/statista/online_dating_market_size.md` → Page 1 / Chart 01

## Signal 3
### ARPU declining across all three subsectors, M dropping fastest

**Fact**: M's ARPU is approximately $30 higher than OD/CD, but M shows the steepest decline of the three; OD shows the slowest.

**Interpretation**: The high-priced serious matchmaking market is losing monetization capacity fastest. The sector overall is "de-monetizing."

**Source**: Statista — Dating Services market data & analysis (Feb 2023)
**Corresponding chart**: Chart 02 — dating-services ARPU (line)
**Secondary evidence**: Chart 07 (M revenue line) shows M with slowest growth — combined with the largest ARPU drop, this constitutes a "double bleeding" pattern
**Corresponding file**: `raw_capture/statista/online_dating_market_size.md` → Page 1 / Chart 02

## Signal 4
### OD ARPU continued decline after 2019 rebound

**Fact**: OD ARPU rose from $7.03 in 2019 to $7.96 in 2021, then declined continuously, with two further small drops in 2023 and 2025.

**Interpretation**: User acquisition decoupled from monetization — users are growing but per-user revenue is dropping. Paywall strategies are approaching marginal failure.

**Source**: Statista — Dating Services market data & analysis (Feb 2023)
**Corresponding chart**: Chart 05 — online-dating ARPU (line)
**Corresponding file**: `raw_capture/statista/online_dating_market_size.md` → Page 2 / Chart 05

## Signal 5
### Business model wall window: 2027–2030

**Fact**: OD growth rate locks at 2% from 2027–2030; M drops from 1.5% to 1.4% starting 2029.

**Interpretation**: Statista's own forecast has built the "wall" into the data — 2030 is the natural endpoint of the current commercial model. This is critical for the 2046 scenario: Statista is not forecasting growth, it is forecasting stagnation.

**Source**: Statista — Dating Services market data & analysis (Feb 2023)
**Corresponding chart**: Chart 04 — online-dating revenue change
**Corresponding file**: `raw_capture/statista/online_dating_market_size.md` → Page 2 / Chart 04

## Signal 6
### Subsector share shifting slowly, not dramatically

**Fact**: From 2017 to 2030, M share dropped from 54.5% to 49.8% (-4.7pp), OD share rose from 31.9% to 39.0% (+7.1pp), CD share dropped from 13.6% to 11.2% (-2.4pp).

**Interpretation**: M is yielding ground to OD, but the migration is small. The narrative "serious matchmaking giving way to casual high-frequency" is directionally correct but weak in magnitude — slow erosion, not structural reversal.

**Source**: Statista — Dating Services market data & analysis (Feb 2023)
**Corresponding chart**: Chart 01 — dating-services revenue (stacked column)
**Corresponding file**: `raw_capture/statista/online_dating_market_size.md` → Page 1 / Chart 01
