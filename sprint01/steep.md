**2046 Dating World — Target State（草稿）**

> By 2046, the dating and relationships landscape has restructured along five interlocking axes.
>
> **Demographic stretch**: With the global median age at 35 and the old-age dependency ratio approaching 24, dating is no longer a one-window pursuit between ages 20 and 35. Adults navigate multiple distinct relationship phases across a 60-to-80-year span, as Monica's classroom prompt of "four relationships in a 120-year lifespan" approaches lived reality. The industry's existing economic model, optimized for a narrow demographic window, faces structural mismatch.
>
> **Geographic redistribution**: 68% of humanity now lives in urban areas, with the largest dating user bases in megacities of the Global South—Lagos, Delhi, Dhaka, Karachi—rather than the New York–London–San Francisco corridor that shaped the industry's first three decades. Product logic, payment infrastructure, and cultural assumptions originally designed for Western urban professionals no longer fit the dominant user.
>
> **Climate compression**: Depending on the emissions trajectory between SSP1-2.6 and SSP3-7.0, the physical environment of dating has shifted significantly. Outdoor social space contracts under heat-humidity extremes; climate-driven displacement undermines the geographic stability long-term relationships once presumed; chronic environmental stress reshapes relationships into co-regulation systems as much as romantic partnerships.
>
> **Regulatory architecture**: Two decades after the EU AI Act reached full implementation, AI presence in intimate life is universal but legally transparent. "AI partner indistinguishable from a human" is not a product category—it is illegal. What exists instead is a stratified regulatory landscape where disclosed-AI companions, human-only dating, and hybrid models occupy distinct legal and market niches.
>
> **Functional fragmentation**: The romantic-monogamous narrative that dominated 20th-century intimacy has fractured into multiple coexisting relationship modes—functional partnership, climate co-coping, transparent AI companionship, traditional romance, and configurations not yet named. This fragmentation is not anomaly but baseline, shaped by a generation raised in chronic loneliness and arriving at intimacy as a practical, lifelong, multi-modal practice rather than a singular life narrative.
