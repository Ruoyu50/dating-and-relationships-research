**对你 dating/relationships 项目最有用的几条：**

一是 **55% → 68%（2018 → 2050）的物理基础变化**：dating 行业的市场基础在加深，不在萎缩。这条和你 Statista "市场撞墙"形成有趣张力——市场不是没人，是用户行为/付费意愿变了。

二是 **新增 2.5 billion 城市居民，90% 在亚非**：dating 行业未来增长不在欧美，在亚非。这条直接挑战 Connection Club 这种"高科技 VR/chip"设定——印度、尼日利亚的城市新增人口是不是 Connection Club 的目标用户？大概率不是。这是不接 artifact，但暗中削弱它的一种方式。

三是 **megacities 从 33 → 43（2018 → 2030）**：超大城市本身就是孤独悖论的极端形态。东京、德里这种规模的城市里，物理密度最大，社会连接最弱。dating app 的存在前提就是城市孤独悖论。

**Urbanization 段（compendium 草稿，仿前两段格式）**

> Urbanization constitutes a third mega driver, providing the physical and social infrastructure within which the 2046 dating landscape will operate. According to the UN World Urbanization Prospects 2018 Revision, the share of the global population living in urban areas is projected to rise from 55% in 2018 to 68% by 2050—continuing a trajectory that has more than doubled since 1950 (UN DESA Population Division, 2018).
>
> The scale and distribution of this growth matter as much as the headline figure. The world is projected to add 2.5 billion urban dwellers by 2050, with nearly 90% of this growth concentrated in Asia and Africa. India alone will add 416 million urban residents, China 255 million, and Nigeria 189 million. By 2030, the world is projected to host 43 megacities of more than 10 million inhabitants, most of them in developing regions.
>
> For dating and relationships, urbanization operates as both opportunity and contradiction. Cities concentrate the high-density anonymous social environment in which app-based dating originally took root—the user base for digital intimate connection continues to expand, particularly outside the saturated markets of North America and Europe. Yet cities also produce the loneliness paradox: the highest physical proximity coupled with the weakest social bonds. The largest dating markets of 2046 will be in megacities of the Global South, where the user demographics, cultural context, and infrastructure assumptions differ sharply from the Western dating model that currently dominates the industry. Whether the industry's existing economic and product logic can serve a Lagos resident, a Delhi resident, or a Dhaka resident as effectively as it has served a New York or London user remains an open question.
