### Chart — Share of AI companion apps users worldwide 2023–2024, by age group (Bar)

- **Research Question:** Who actually uses AI companion apps, and what is the age distribution?

- **The Facts:**
  - **18–24:** 65.4% of users
  - **25–34:** 12.5%
  - **35–49:** 8.3%
  - **50–64:** 13.3%
  - **65+:** 0.4%
  - **Concentration:** Two-thirds of all users are under 25; users over 50 are 13.7% combined; seniors are statistically negligible.

- **Synthesis/Interpretation:**
  AI companion adoption is a **young-user phenomenon**. The 18–24 cohort dominates with 65.4% share, while users over 50 combined account for less than 14%. This skews demographically opposite to the dating-app cohort, which is aging toward median 35 (per UN WPP 2024 demographic data).

  Two populations are running in parallel rather than in sequence: a young cohort adopts AI companions as default intimacy infrastructure, while the legacy dating-app cohort ages independently. This is a demographic divergence, not a substitution pattern.

## Open Questions

- **Causality:** Why is the 50–64 bracket (13.3%) higher than 25–34 (12.5%) and 35–49 (8.3%)? Possible U-shaped adoption (young + lonely-older) — but data does not disaggregate by loneliness, household status, or care burden. Unverified.
- **Definitions:** Appfigures categorizes "AI companion apps" by app store taxonomy — borderline cases (e.g., Replika vs. general chatbots like Character.AI) may be inconsistently classified.
- **Missing Data:** Gender breakdown, geographic distribution, intent-of-use (companionship vs. romance vs. friendship) not in this chart.

## Workflow Notes

### Methodological Refinement
- **Optimization:** Statista's chart-level granularity is high but disaggregated data lives across multiple separate charts. Age + intent + geographic charts should be captured together where they exist as a coordinated dataset (rare on Statista).
- **Key Indicators:** Age distribution proved most valuable because it directly tests whether AI companion adoption follows the same demographic curve as dating-app decline. Other charts in the search (downloads, MAU) measure adoption velocity, not user identity.
