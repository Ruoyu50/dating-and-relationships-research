**2046 落在哪个窗口？**

SPM 没有直接给 2041-2060 单独区间，但 B.1.1 + B.1.2 说："In the near term (2021-2040), global warming is more likely than not to reach 1.5°C even under SSP1-1.9, and likely or very likely to exceed 1.5°C under higher scenarios. Discernible differences between contrasting scenarios begin to emerge within ~20 years."

翻译成对你有用的话：到 2046（中期窗口），不同 SSP 路径已经明显分化。这意味着 2046 的世界长什么样，**取决于 2026-2046 这 20 年人类做了什么**。

这正是 backcasting 框架的逻辑——未来不是预测出来的，是选择的结果。

**Climate 接到 dating/relationships 的因果链（候选）**

我从 SPM 里提取出三条最能接到主题的因果链：

**链条一：Heat-humidity → 户外社交场景萎缩**

SPM B.2.1 + Figure SPM.3(b)：升温到 2.4-3.1°C，全球面临"对人类有致死风险的高温高湿天数"显著增加，部分地区从历史 0-50 天升至 150-300 天/年。

接 dating：约会场景历来高度依赖户外（公园、街头、餐厅露台、城市步行）。当一年中三分之一时间室外存在 mortality risk，dating 的物理空间假设被打破。"在家见面"或"室内/虚拟空间见面"从偏好变成必需。

**链条二：Climate-driven displacement → 关系不稳定性**

SPM A.2.5 + B.2.4：气候和极端天气在非洲、亚洲、北美、中南美洲已造成 displacement，未来加剧。3.3-3.6 billion 人生活在高脆弱性区域。

接 dating：长期关系传统上依赖地理稳定性（同城、同社区）。当人口被气候推动迁移成为常态，"长期同地"假设瓦解。远距离关系、临时性关系、跨地域关系结构会变成主流而非例外。

**链条三：Climate anxiety + mental health → 关系功能变化**

SPM A.2.5：mental health challenges associated with increasing temperatures (high confidence)、trauma from extreme events (very high confidence)、loss of livelihoods and culture (high confidence)。

接 dating：心理健康负担增加 → 关系不仅是浪漫，更承担情感支持/共同应对气候焦虑的功能 → "找一个能一起扛"的择偶逻辑可能比"找一个浪漫"更突出。

**Climate 段（compendium 草稿，仿 WPP 那段格式）**

> Climate change forms a second mega driver shaping the 2046 dating landscape, operating not as a single trajectory but as a branching set of possibilities. According to IPCC AR6 (2023), global temperature rise by 2081-2100 is projected to range from 1.4°C under the strongest mitigation scenario (SSP1-1.9) to 4.4°C under the highest emissions pathway (SSP5-8.5). By the mid-century mark of 2046, these scenarios have already begun to diverge meaningfully (IPCC AR6 SPM, Section B.1).
>
> The five SSP scenarios themselves constitute a research-grounded cone of plausibility: SSP1-1.9 and SSP1-2.6 represent preferred futures where coordinated mitigation succeeds; SSP2-4.5 represents the projected baseline under current trajectories; SSP3-7.0 and SSP5-8.5 represent preposterous but not implausible futures of mitigation failure.
>
> What climate change means for dating and relationships extends beyond environmental backdrop. Three structural shifts emerge across scenarios: first, increasing days per year of dangerous heat-humidity conditions (rising from historical baselines to 150-300 days annually under 2.4-3.1°C warming) compress outdoor social spaces, forcing relationship formation increasingly into indoor or mediated environments. Second, climate-driven displacement, already affecting populations across Africa, Asia, North America, and Central/South America, undermines the geographic stability that long-term relationships have historically presumed. Third, climate-related mental health burdens shift the functional role of intimate relationships from primarily romantic to increasingly supportive—partners as co-regulators of chronic environmental stress.
>
> For the dating and relationships industry, this means scenario design cannot assume a single climate future. The world of 2046 may be one where climate stability allows traditional dating models to persist with adaptation, or one where climate disruption has fundamentally restructured how, where, and with whom humans form intimate bonds.
