# §2.1 Futures Wheel — Output

## Section 1 — §2.1 Futures Wheel Narrative

This Futures Wheel follows Jerome Glenn's canonical methodology, in which a central driver radiates outward through first-, second-, and third-order consequences as a radial mind map (Glenn 1972; for contemporary summaries see Swanson 2020 and Kemp 2025). The driver at the center is the dating industry's 2027–2030 de-monetization wall: ARPU declines across every subsector while user count grows (Statista, *Dating Services — Worldwide*, 2026); EU AI Act Articles 5(1)(b) and 50 expose engagement-on-loneliness algorithms to legal liability "by effect" (Regulation 2024/1689); and the median user ages past the 20–35 product window toward 35.21 by 2046 (UN World Population Prospects 2024). We extend the standard wheel format in two deliberate ways. First, third-order consequences are evaluated against a "systemic restructuring" criterion adapted from Voros's (2003) Futures Cone literature on category-creating shifts, rather than treated as intensified second-order effects — this preserves a clean handoff into §2.3. Second, each third-order node is tagged to a Cone scenario as a research-to-artifact cohesion device, not a Glenn requirement.

The first ring is STEEP-balanced rather than linear; the five direct consequences deliberately compete. [A] *AI-companion substitution* is the technological branch — but the demographic structure complicates a simple substitution narrative. Between 2023 and 2024, 65.4% of AI companion app users were aged 18–24, with users over 50 accounting for only 13.7% combined (Statista / Appfigures 2024). The dating-app median user, meanwhile, is aging toward 35 (UN WPP 2024). The two populations run in parallel rather than sequence: a generationally distinct cohort adopts AI companions as default intimacy infrastructure, while the legacy dating-app cohort ages out. This generational misalignment feeds Ring 3's stratification consequence (node 3) along a second axis beyond income and jurisdiction. [B] *Outdoor sociality compresses* under environmental and spatial pressure, with 150–300 dangerous heat-humidity days per year projected for target megacities under SSP2-4.5 to SSP3-7.0 (IPCC 2023, SPM Figure SPM.3; see also Xue, Chen, and Yu 2025 on sustainable aviation trajectories). [C] An *EU regulatory floor on AI-mediated intimacy* emerges from Article 50 disclosure and the Article 5(1)(b) "by effect" prohibition on exploitation of vulnerability (Regulation 2024/1689). [D] Economically, *surviving platforms consolidate post-wall into a barbell of tiered jurisdictional pricing* — premium verified-human against cheap unverified AI (Statista 2026 revenue-wall forecast). [E] Socially and demographically, *the dating lifespan lengthens*: median age 31→35 and a 49% rise in the old-age dependency ratio break the 20–35 product logic (UN WPP 2024).

The second ring traces reactive forces, not smooth extrapolation. Branches A and C combine in two opposing directions: under a holding Brussels Effect, regulation legitimates AI companionship across roughly 38 states and 71% of the dating market (Bradford 2020); under fragmentation, the same regulatory pressure drives engagement-optimization into offshore gray-market AI — Bradford's inverse case. Branch B's heat compression forces indoor commercial venues to become the substrate for intimate sociality (IPCC AR6 SPM). Aviation's growth trajectory consumes nearly a quarter of the 1.5°C carbon budget by 2050 (Plucinska 2025; Dobruszkes, Mattioli, and Gozzoli 2024), making long-distance romantic mobility a contested climate-policy variable in its own right. Branch C's compliance burden displaces small operators in favor of incumbents able to absorb Article 27 FRIA documentation and Article 50 disclosure costs (Regulation 2024/1689). Branch D collapses the mid-market and leaves a barbell of premium verified-human against cheap unverified AI (Statista 2026). Branch E reframes loneliness from individual complaint to population-health framing as working-age cohorts manage longer dating lifespans under heavier care burden (UN WPP 2024; IPCC AR6 SPM A.2.5 flags mental-health load with high confidence; Khatib 2023 on climate-travel health intersections).

Six third-order consequences pass the systemic-restructuring test. (1) *Intimacy infrastructure becomes a public-goods question* — the same physical typology answered publicly or privately (→ Projected + Preferred, the same question with opposite answers). (2) *The dating industry merges with the care economy* as loneliness becomes doctor-prescribed and insurance-covered (→ Preferred — Care Commons). (3) *Two-tier intimacy stratification by jurisdiction and income* — premium verified-human in one tier, gray-market AI in the other (→ Projected — Tiered World; Preposterous — Two-Tier World). (4) *Climate-adaptive indoor commons replace open-air sociality*, with ownership varying across privatized, public-cooperative, and gray-market models (→ all three cones at different ownership switches). (5) *Chronic loneliness reclassified as public-health infrastructure*, co-funded by health systems (→ Preferred — Care Commons). (6) *Aviation collapse contracts the geography of romance*, pushing cross-regional intimacy into VR/simulation (Santoso et al. 2025 on VR reducing psychological distance to climate-affected locations), anchored in the Companion Wars wildcard from §2.3 (→ Preposterous — Two-Tier World).

The rings are not a one-way cascade. Four feedback loops generate different Cone outcomes depending on which way the switches flip: AI-engagement loops back onto regulation as licensure-versus-offshoring; de-monetization loops through consolidation and exclusion into the public-goods fork; climate compression loops through indoor commercialization into the commons-or-tier fork; and demographic aging loops through public-health framing into the care-economy merger. The wheel maps the driver across all three cones; the Connection Club artifact occupies the Preposterous branch, specifically the intersection of node 3 (stratification) and node 6 (aviation collapse + mediated cross-regional intimacy). Climate did not cause the stratification — climate forced the question, and the regulatory answer at each loop decides which cone the wheel resolves into.
