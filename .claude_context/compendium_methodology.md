During the presentation, Prof. Belot raised a pointed question: 
"If no one travels in this world, why would anyone go to a facility 
to experience VR?" This exposed a structural contradiction between 
the Preposterous cone (mobility collapse, region-locked life) and 
the artifact Connection Club (which requires physical facility visits).

The compendium MUST resolve this tension explicitly in §2.6. Use this 
framing (integrate naturally, do not paste verbatim):

> Air travel collapsed, but intra-regional mobility persists via 
> expanded rail and local transit. Connection Club facilities are 
> regional infrastructure — accessible within 60–90 minutes of major 
> urban centers, but only within one's region of residence. 
> Cross-regional travel for intimacy is no longer possible. Additionally, 
> post-Companion Wars regulation classified at-home immersive identity 
> systems as too high-risk to license — physical facility deployment, 
> with mandated biometric audit and session logging, became the only 
> legal channel for regulated intimate AI. The friction of going to a 
> facility is not a usability flaw; it is a regulatory feature. Combined 
> with €7,200 annual fees, this physical access requirement becomes part 
> of the stratification the cone reveals: only those near a facility 
> and able to afford the trip become members.
